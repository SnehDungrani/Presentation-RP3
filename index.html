~/Inter-Act/03-orientation

What you are about to see

Watch the actors, not a controller

A plain-English goal goes in. Autonomous actors - robotic arms and AGVs - bid for the work on a shared message bus. No central controller decides who does what.

Kill an actor mid-run and no controller steps in to fix it. Another actor notices on the message bus and negotiates to take over the task.

~/Inter-Act/05-architecture

The real system · architecture

Centralized goal, decentralized execution

click to zoom ⤢
Interpreter / Announcer (strategic) decentralized (tactical + recovery) peer coordination on the bus
Hybrid by design. The operator sets a goal; a central interpreter and announcer turn it into an auction on the shared bus. From there, execution, actor-led fault detection, and recovery run across the actors - the recovery claim is authored by another actor, not a central controller. Each actor is its own container; the 0.5B narrator only writes operator bubbles.

~/Inter-Act/06-coordination

How coordination works

Bidding decides. The LLM only advises.

1. announce on bus 2. AGVs bid by themselves 3. claim winner via bus
mechanism Actors bid on a shared bus; the MasterAnnouncer picks and claims the winner.
decides Distance, capability, and workload scores determine which actor wins the bid.
advises only The LLM adjusts bid scores within fixed limits - it never overrides the decision.

~/Inter-Act/07-validation

Validate · LIVE + simulation

It works - with one known edge

0.0 s ±2.2 s
1 package · goal → delivered · mean of 5 runs
0.0 s ±7.2 s
3 packages · goal → all delivered · mean of 5 runs

end to end - goal in, plan, bid, move and deliver; the time scales with the task

recovery

When an actor fails, another actor - not a central controller - picks up its task: spotted on the bus in 9.24 s, re-allocated in 20.28 s, recovered in all 10 of 10 fault-injection runs.

allocation

In simulation, the bid score (skills, distance, workload) picks a near-optimal actor for each task.

the open edge

A package mid-carry is lost when its actor crashes - the takeover actor has nothing to pick up. A small fix, not architectural. (see future work)

Future work

Where INTER.act is heading

▸ close the gap

Complete the recovery

  • Release locks on heartbeat loss so re-allocated actors finish delivery.
  • Faults recover end to end, not just re-allocation.
▸ truly contested

Beyond single-bidder

  • Move past the single eligible bidder toward genuinely distributed allocation.
  • More actors, real competition on the bus.
▸ UI · build it

Visual builder

  • Parallel routes and per-actor parameters authored from the canvas.
▸ beyond the warehouse

Any multi-agent system

  • The warehouse is one scenario - the coordination foundation is domain-agnostic.
  • Drone displays (one drone drops out), underwater cleaning fleets, defence and search-and-rescue swarms.
